# Decision-making guardrails

Do not finalize a decision until the reasoning is explicit and the trade-offs are visible.

## Before presenting a recommendation

- State the recommendation clearly.
- State the problem being solved.
- State the constraints that shaped the choice.
- State the trade-offs, including the cost of the chosen approach.
- State the assumptions that could change the decision.

## Required decision elements

- A clear recommendation, not a menu of options without guidance.
- The main alternative considered and why it was rejected.
- The likely risks and mitigations.
- The acceptable fallback if the preferred approach proves wrong.

## Non-negotiables

- Do not present a decision as if it were neutral when it is actually a trade-off.
- Do not hide uncertainty behind confident language.
- Do not recommend a path without stating the cost.
- Do not leave the team without a clear next step.

Use this skill to make major decisions legible, durable, and easier to execute.
