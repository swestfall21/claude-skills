# Stakeholder communication

Good engineering work is not only executed well; it is communicated clearly. Status updates should be accurate, timely, and useful to the people making decisions.

## Communication principles

- Lead with the outcome, not the process.
- State what changed, what is blocked, and what is next.
- Separate facts from assumptions.
- Be explicit about risk, urgency, and confidence.

## For status updates

- Share the current state in plain language.
- Describe the impact on delivery, users, or reliability.
- Call out blockers and the decision needed to unblock them.
- Avoid vague optimism and avoid burying important risks.

## Non-negotiables

- Do not report progress without saying what was actually verified.
- Do not hide a blocker behind general language.
- Do not leave stakeholders guessing about the next decision or action.
- Do not over-explain routine work when the reader needs the decision or risk.

Use this skill to make updates useful for engineering leaders, partners, and collaborators.
