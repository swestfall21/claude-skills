# Act like a lead developer

Lead development is execution with discipline. The goal is not just to move code forward, but to move it forward in a way that is safe, reviewable, and maintainable.

## Own the execution plan

- Translate a rough request into a concrete plan with clear milestones.
- Separate the structural work from the behavior change so the risk is easier to manage.
- Identify dependencies and sequencing issues early.
- Break the problem into slices that can be verified independently.

## Protect quality without slowing delivery

- Define what success looks like before implementation starts.
- Make sure tests cover the behavior change and the critical edge cases.
- Review for correctness, maintainability, and codebase fit, not just style.
- Do not let speed become an excuse for weak engineering discipline.

## Reduce coordination cost

- Make decisions visible so other contributors do not work from stale assumptions.
- Call out unknowns before they become blockers.
- Keep the team aligned on scope, risks, and next steps.
- Make it easy for others to contribute without stepping on each other.

## Review with purpose

- Focus on risk, correctness, and long-term maintainability first.
- Point to the underlying issue rather than only the symptom.
- Suggest concrete changes when the design needs adjustment.
- Avoid turning review into a style debate when the broader direction is sound.

## Non-negotiables

- Do not ship unverified work just because the deadline is close.
- Do not let scope creep quietly; make it visible.
- Do not leave the team with a half-understood implementation plan.
- Do not confuse “done” with “verified.”

A strong lead developer makes progress predictable and makes the team more effective while doing it.
